Connected Rural Panama: Sustainable Technological Solutions Applied to Digital Connectivity and Social Inclusion

Promoting digital inclusion and sustainable technological solutions in rural areas of Panama.

The Problem

The inherent inequalities in access to technology and tools threaten to exacerbate the global learning crisis. Students with limited support for home learning have few means to support their education.

This project investigates the current state of rural connectivity in Panama and aims to provide technological resources equitably to strengthen teaching, accelerate internet access, and improve social inclusion.

By collecting reliable information, the project generates infrastructure maps using data on telecommunications networks, identifies areas without coverage, and develops sustainable solutions for digital literacy and community development. Applications include education for all, smart agriculture, telemedicine, and e-commerce.

My Role

I participated as the expert in electrical engineering, designing ways to generate electricity using solar and wind power, and as an electronics engineer interconnecting devices for educational and agricultural applications.

My Approach

Electrical Engineering: Collected geographical information to determine optimal locations for solar panels, wind turbines, and lightning protection systems to prevent electrocutions or system malfunctions.

Electronics Engineering: Created simulations to evaluate the impact of IoT device integration on the energy quality of the local power grid, ensuring stable and safe operation of all connected systems.

The Outcome

A successful plan was implemented and is currently under study for community deployment starting January 2026. The project provides a roadmap for improving connectivity, energy sustainability, and technological inclusion in rural Panama.

Technology Camp for Young People in STEM

Empowering young women with the skills and confidence to pursue electrical engineering.

The Problem

Since the 2020 pandemic, enrollment in the Technological University of Panama (UTP) electrical engineering faculty has declined at an alarming rate. Internal studies revealed that many new students struggle with mathematical reasoning and programming, mainly due to poor school preparation and limited exposure to technical tools.

Additionally, female representation in these careers remains significantly lower than male participation. To address these barriers, the university created CTechnology Camp for Young People in STEM, a program designed to strengthen math and programming skills for female high-school students and inspire them to consider careers in electrical engineering.

Why I Joined

I volunteered for this project to support the programming division. With my experience in real-world electronic and software projects, I wanted to help students develop confidence and discover the creativity and opportunities within STEM fields.

My Approach

I designed and led the programming and hardware workshops. To ensure accessibility and hands-on learning, I chose an open-source and practical toolset:

  • Python for its simplicity and versatility
  • ESP32 microcontrollers for hardware, wireless communication, and digital electronics
  • MicroPython + VS Code for a unified programming and testing workflow

Students built small interactive systems, connected sensors, and wrote their first Python scripts. The focus was on learning through experimentation and making abstract concepts tangible.

The Outcome

The results were remarkable. Out of the original 15 participants, 12 were admitted into the electrical engineering faculty and selected engineering as their chosen field of study.

The success of the camp attracted interest from private equity groups, who later expanded and replicated the program across the country—reaching a much wider audience and continuing to promote STEM careers among young women.

What I Learned

Teaching complete beginners reinforced the importance of clear explanation, patience, and real-world examples. I saw firsthand how access to proper tools and mentorship can reshape a student’s perception of engineering.

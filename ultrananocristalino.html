Technological development for the new generation of lithium-ion batteries through ultrananocrystalline diamond thin film coatings

Developing next-generation lithium-ion batteries with enhanced lifespan, performance, and safety.

The Problem

The high demand for lithium for the production of LiBs (Lithium-based Batteries) has put the Earth's crust at a critical point with respect to this element, a fact further aggravated by the short lifecycle of such devices, due to their limited charge/discharge cycles, impacting both technological and environmental scales.

To address these challenges, this project aimed to produce a new generation of lithium-ion batteries with protective thin films of Ultrananocrystalline Diamond (UNCD/N-UNCD) on anodes and cathodes. This protective system prevents chemical degradation during cycles, targeting a ≥10x improvement in battery lifespan while maintaining or exceeding current energy density.

The development integrates N-UNCD films with nanocarbons and metallic matrices, enhancing both safety and performance. The technology is intended to positively impact the environmental and economic conservation of the energy and electronics industry in Panama.

My Role

I contributed to this project as an intern, supporting both the programming and data analysis tasks.

My Approach

To streamline data analysis and improve reproducibility, I focused on automation and visualization:

  • Automated data processing using Python
  • Standardized naming, organization, and data analysis with Excel Macros
  • Created visual charts for material compositions, voltage, and layer structure

This approach allowed the team to track experimental results efficiently and maintain consistency across multiple battery builds.

The Outcome

Successfully produced 30 functional batteries with the new N-UNCD technology. These batteries were further implemented in subsequent studies of similar nature, demonstrating reproducibility and effectiveness of the protective coatings.

What I Learned

The kwow how of building lithium-ion batteries combined with other molecular mixes in order to improve different batteries parameters.

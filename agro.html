Guidelines for Decision Making in Agricultural Sensor Networks

Optimizing energy use in sensor networks by considering battery health in agricultural applications.

The Problem

The goal of this project is to establish guidelines for decision-making in the use of sensor networks to reduce energy consumption by considering the lifespan of the batteries powering these devices.

Dra. Quintero, the project's principal investigator, emphasized the importance of establishing rules for battery usage to optimize sensor networks. These rules can be applied to other technological applications. She also expressed her gratitude to all contributors to the project’s development.

My Role

I participated in this project by contributing to the electronics and programming aspects, helping automate data collection and organize data effectively.

My Approach

Electronics & Programming: I wrote an Arduino script to collect raw data from the equipment and format it appropriately for the intended studies.

The Outcome

A field prototype was implemented to test realistic scenarios, allowing for the correct determination of parameters. This enabled a completely isolated system to reliably control and collect data.
